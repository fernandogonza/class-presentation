1865 - The Foundation of the KKK

Shortly after the Civil War ended, the KKK was first formed. Confederate soliders and general formed a secret group in Pulaski, Tennesse. In order to uphold the beliefs of white supremacy in southern states, but most importantly in America. Causing a large campaign to expand throughout the states.

1870 - 1882 , Dimmishing the KKK

After noticing the increase in violence revovling around the KKK group, the United States finally made actions to disband the group. In 1970, congress passed the FORCE ACT, in order to give rights to blacks with the 14th & 15th Amendments. 1971, the KU KLUX KLAN ACT was formed. This provided the president to combat attacks against black rights. Finally, in 1882, United States V. Harris declared the KKK as unconstitutional. The end of the first Ku Klux Klan wave.

1915 - The Birth of a Nation

The revival of the KKK can be linked back to the film THE BIRTH OF A NATION. A pro-white supremency and patriotical film about defending southern beliefs, dirested by D.W. Griffith. The Film also inspired the White Knight persona and hooded costume for the KKK.

1918 - WEDDING BAND

The year of when WEDDING BAND happens. During this time, the Klan is rapidly gaining members. This willl creates an increase in hate crimes, murder, fear, and violence.

1920's - The Klans Peak

At it's peak, the klan exceeded over 4 million members nation-wide. Burning cross also starts to become the symbol of the orginization. The group also welcomes people who are against Roman Catholics, Jews, foreigners and organized labour. Instead of the general hate against blacks, immigrants, communist, and whites who supports race mixing.

1928 - The Law Against the KKK Violence

Up till the 1928, anyone who participated in lynching. Was able to do so freely, as it was legal. The ANTI-LYNCHING LAW came into effect. However, since the law was based on state-offense. Southern states had the option to convict or not. No white person was ever convicted for committing crimes against African Americans.
